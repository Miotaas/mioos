Command Center — Layout Approach

Three ways to give it a morning brief feel. Which direction resonates?

A

The Briefing Board

A top banner says "Good morning — here's what happened while you were away." Below it, a horizontal strip of 4–5 critical KPI pills (MRR, open approvals, active departments, top opportunity score). Then a 3-column grid: left = Revenue + Goals, center = Opportunities pipeline + Recent discoveries, right = Approvals needed + Blockers + Executive intelligence. The morning brief header auto-summarizes the last N hours of activity in 2–3 bullet points.

B

The Intelligence Feed

Left column = a chronological "while you were away" feed — ranked by importance, with timestamps. Research discoveries, approvals generated, assignments completed, revenue movements, risks flagged. Right column = live company vitals (2 narrower cards stacked: Revenue status, Workforce status). The feed feels like opening a Slack channel about your company. Newest event at top, color-coded by department. Scroll to go further back.

C

Hybrid: Brief Header + Live Grid

Best of both. A slim, premium "morning brief" header (date, time away, 2–3 auto-generated intelligence bullets in a dark gradient card — feels like an executive briefing). Then immediately below: the live company grid from the existing Command Center (Revenue, Opportunities, Projects, Approvals) but enhanced with Workforce section and Executive Intelligence panel. No column for a feed — the header carries the "what changed" story, the grid carries the live state.
