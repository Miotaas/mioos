MioOS V3 — Idea Lifecycle

Where does an idea live before it becomes a project and before it needs a decision?

The tension: Decide must stay clean — only things needing an active go/no-go right now.
Projects must stay committed — no half-baked concepts polluting the view.
So where do raw ideas, discoveries, and early-stage signals live?

Three models below. Each gives a different answer.

Linear Funnel Problem
Discovery → Opportunity → Decision → Project (each lives in a separate page)

Problem: This is what MioOS currently does. Four separate surfaces means opportunity data gets siloed in "Opportunities", never integrated with the projects that came from them. The founder navigates a pipeline instead of a system. And "Opportunities" as a standalone page is the exact problem we identified earlier — the same data lives in multiple places.

Why this works: A raw signal stays in Teams until the founder consciously decides to track it. That single decision creates an "Exploring" project. All pre-decision work lives in Projects (just at low commitment stages). Decide only enters when validation is complete and a formal go/no-go is ready. Decide never becomes an idea backlog. Projects never has uncommitted noise.
Model C
Teams-First Too hidden
Everything lives in Teams until it's ready for a formal decision. Projects only for committed work.

Problem: The founder can't see what's being explored without diving into Teams. There's no way to browse and curate the pipeline. Ideas being tracked feel invisible. Teams becomes a black box rather than a dispatch layer. This model works only if the founder trusts the AI to surface everything — which is a Level 4+ autonomy assumption.

Recommended answer: Model B

Signal (untracked)
Lives in Teams → Discoveries. Raw AI finding. The founder has not said "track this" yet. May be dismissed or promoted.
Exploring
Lives in Projects at stage "Exploring". Created the moment the founder says "track this". Minimal data: name, one-liner, confidence low. No tasks yet.
Validating
Lives in Projects at stage "Validating". AI teams assigned research tasks. Validation plan active. Still no commitment to build.
Ready to decide
AI team produces full proposal → enters Decide as a proposal. This is the ONLY moment it enters Decide. Not before.
Approved
Returns to Projects at stage "Building". Now committed. Tasks, revenue tracking, milestones.
Rejected
Project moves to Archived in Projects. History preserved. Can be revisited later.
The critical rule: Decide is not a pipeline. Decide is a decision point.
Something enters Decide when — and only when — it has been validated enough that a yes/no answer is appropriate.
Before that moment: it lives in Projects (Exploring or Validating stage).
After that moment: it returns to Projects (Building or Archived stage).
Projects owns the full lifecycle. Decide owns the moment of judgment.
